C++ Graphics Engine

I built, from the ground up, a rendering graphics engine in C++ using OpenCL and homemade kernels. My OpenCL kernels run efficiently on an AMD, Nvidia, or Intel graphics cards through a CL driver.

The program will take in, either, an .obj file or procedurally create primitive graphics at runtime.

The above image is comprised of 100 cats, each with over 30 thousand vertices rendered at 30 frames per second on my laptop's graphics card.

Socialsteam

I am currently creating a new type of social media designed to get people off of their phones and into real life. I am doing this by creating a comprehensive user experience that matches users to real events where they can find new people and have fun.

Users may sign up for an account and, either, post their own events or search for things to do on the website. The website creates a connection graph between events which allows people to find events related to the ones they already are going to.

Banana Pie

At Tensentric, I collaborated with many engineering disciplines to develop a wireless variant of the popular Banana Pie AAC switch device using Bluetooth Low Energy on an ESP32 Microcontroller. I worked cross-functionally to create multi-threaded, embedded C code using Bluetooth and HID over GATT.

I helped define project requirements in client-facing meetings, and was the person to suggest BLE as the communication protocol, having brought in a small proof-of-concept device from home to show the team.

Skills Developed
  • Embedded Software Development
  • Zephyr RTOS, JTAG debugging, HID over GATT, Bluetooth Low Energy, Cross-functional Teamwork
